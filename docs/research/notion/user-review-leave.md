# Notion: Why Users Leave

Based on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Reddit, Medium, and UX Planet (2024-2026).

## 1. Steep Learning Curve & Overwhelming Complexity
- "Vast features make it daunting for newcomers"
- "Users spend more time learning and configuring than getting work done"
- "The platform's power can backfire" — "layered versatility" creates a "monster" system
- Weeks or months mastering databases, formulas, workflows
- "Setup was pretty difficult compared to Monday which is very intuitive"

## 2. "Jack of All Trades, Master of None"
- Tables can't match Excel for advanced calculations
- PM features can't match Jira or Asana for large projects
- "Too complex for simplest needs, not powerful enough for most complex needs"
- "Stuck in the middle" feeling frustrates users
- Specialized tools outperform in every individual category

## 3. Performance & Speed Degradation
- Lag with large, interconnected databases
- "It's often buggy and slow now, making managing tasks frustrating"
- "As workspaces grow, performance can slow down"
- Power users with complex setups report significant lag

## 4. UI & Editing Quirks
- "Probably the most frustrating document tool I've ever used" — Capterra
- Editing near @mentions pulls up profiles instead of allowing text editing
- Pasting text messes up formatting: extra spaces, weird lists
- Lists and indents work differently from expectations
- Comments pushed far from original marked-up location in long documents

## 5. Aggressive/Opaque AI Pricing
- Plus plan subscribers told they'd "run out of free AI responses" after 2 questions
- 20 lifetime AI responses on Plus (same as Free) — not disclosed at subscription
- "Changed pricing model in May 2025 to force users onto expensive plans"
- Previously could add AI for $8/month to any plan; now bundled only in Business ($20/month)
- "All-or-nothing" — must pay for every user in workspace

## 6. Billing & Customer Support
- "Worst billing experience I've ever encountered" — billed for users never added
- "Nasty habit of attempting to charge piecemeal for lots of interlinking services"
- "Zero help from support" when encountering errors
- Trustpilot complaints about unexpected charges and difficult cancellation

## 7. Sorting, Filtering & Display Issues
- Alphabetical default ordering breaks chronological planning (April, August, May...)
- Comment display pushed away from original location in long documents
- Filter UX not intuitive for complex queries

## 8. Non-Technical Teams Struggle
- "If your team just wants a tool that works out of the box, Notion might cause more frustration"
- "Permissions and complex databases are a headache to support"
- "New editors must be taught not to kill views or duplicate fields"
- Requires willingness to build and tinker — not plug-and-play

## 9. Forms & Database Construction
- "Forms part needs to be easier when building"
- "Constructing a database through a form is more complex when selecting fields"
- Database setup requires understanding schema design concepts

## 10. AI Connector Limitations
- 36-72 hour sync delay for external tool connections
- Read-only: can search but not act on external data
- No real-time external queries
- Short memory across conversations

## Why Users Actually Leave (Medium Article Analysis)
"Why Users Abandon Notion" — top reasons clustered:
1. **Complexity fatigue**: More time configuring than doing actual work
2. **Performance at scale**: Large workspaces become sluggish
3. **Middle ground frustration**: Not simple enough for basics, not powerful enough for advanced
4. **Pricing changes**: Aggressive AI pricing pushes cost-conscious users away
5. **Rise of AI alternatives**: New tools purpose-built for AI-first workflows

## Key Churn Insight
Notion's churn mirrors its strengths: the flexibility that attracts users also overwhelms them. Teams that invest in a "Notion architect" (someone who designs and maintains the workspace) succeed; teams that expect everyone to figure it out organically end up with chaos and abandonment. The May 2025 pricing changes created a secondary churn wave among cost-sensitive users.
