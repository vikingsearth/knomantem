# Confluence: Why Users Leave

Based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, Software Advice, TrustRadius, Reddit, Dev.to, and Medium (2024-2026).

## 1. Performance & Speed (Top Complaint — 66 G2 mentions)
- Slow page loads, especially with large pages and heavy attachments
- Performance degrades noticeably in large spaces (500+ pages)
- Page loads can take 5-10 seconds in complex instances
- Slow with embedded media or on lower-bandwidth connections
- "The platform can feel sluggish, particularly when handling larger documents or multiple integrations"

## 2. Non-Intuitive Navigation & UI (68 G2 mentions)
- Steep learning curve for new users
- Navigation and formatting often prove challenging and frustrating
- Editing experience described as slow and complex
- "Navigating through large spaces with many pages and subpages can be challenging"
- Users can't find documents when structure isn't well-standardized
- The two universal complaints: "I never know where to find what I'm looking for" and "I never know where I should put something new"

## 3. Search Quality
- "Search can be hit-or-miss" — consistently cited pain point
- Finding content often requires browsing hierarchies instead of quick recall
- Engineers especially frustrated by search requiring manual browsing
- Editing and finding files directly isn't seamless — "you often need to download and re-upload"
- Search results don't always surface the most relevant content
- Large instances with many spaces exacerbate the problem

## 4. Content Organization & Stale Content (54 G2 mentions)
- Spaces inevitably fill with duplicate, outdated, and poorly organized content
- "I have never seen a Confluence space that wasn't absolutely packed with tons of irrelevant, outdated, poorly organized piles of miscellaneous information" — Reddit user
- No strong incentives or mechanisms to clean up old content
- Teams create content but don't maintain it
- Information graveyards erode trust in the knowledge base
- "Confluence is where information goes to die" — Dev.to article title

## 5. Editor & Formatting Issues
- Copy-paste from external sources breaks formatting unexpectedly
- "The editor has a lot of formatting issues and limited designing elements and is very restricting"
- PDF export produces messy formatting requiring manual fixes
- Limited control over page layout and design
- Compared unfavorably to Notion's flexible block-based editor

## 6. Confusing Permissions (50 G2 mentions)
- Space vs. page permissions are confusing to set up
- Configuring proper access for contractors requires significant admin time
- Version control alongside permissions adds complexity
- "Confluence's complexity daunting, particularly with sharing permissions, searching documents, and managing version control"

## 7. Steep Learning Curve (53 G2 mentions)
- Not intuitive for new users to pick up
- Interface and setup process described as confusing and time-consuming
- "Either there is not much consideration given to usability or it has now so many bells and whistles that simple things are not simple any more"
- Teams often need dedicated training or champions to drive adoption
- Low adoption rates when rollout isn't carefully managed

## 8. Poor Mobile & Offline Experience
- Mobile app is barely functional for editing
- Viewing on mobile is acceptable but editing is clunky
- No offline access — problematic for traveling or during internet outages
- Falls behind competitors (Notion, Obsidian) in mobile experience

## 9. Real-Time Collaboration Gaps
- Real-time editing exists but is clunky compared to Google Docs or Notion
- Whiteboards not as intuitive as dedicated tools (Miro, FigJam)
- Some teams use Confluence only as point-in-time references, collaborating elsewhere
- Feels dated compared to modern collaborative editors

## 10. Licensing Costs at Scale
- Per-user pricing balloons quickly for teams of 75+ users
- Enterprise features (analytics, automation, content manager) locked behind Premium tier
- "Management may complain about the cost of Confluence and Jira"
- Migration to cloud from self-hosted Server added unexpected costs for many organizations

## 11. Atlassian's Strategic Shifts
- End of Confluence Server support pushes customers toward cloud/data center
- Steady subscription price increases
- AI features add complexity and cost
- "For many organizations, this shift has been a tipping point"
- Forced migration creates natural evaluation moments where teams consider alternatives

## 12. Overengineering
- Tries to be an all-in-one solution but falls short in key areas
- "More and more teams find Confluence too overengineered and slow to be used effectively"
- Leads to out-of-date content and low engagement
- Rigid structure means heavy investment in page architecture or you end up with duplication and dead links

## Clustered Pain Points (from aggregated unhappy reviews)
Ranked by frequency:
1. Too complex/difficult to use (15 mentions)
2. UI complaints (9)
3. Too difficult to find information (6)
4. Limited features or capabilities (4)
5. Lack of integration (4)
6. Too general/not specialized (3)
7. Resource intensive (1)
8. Security concerns (1)

## Key Churn Insight
Teams leave Confluence when the content graveyard problem becomes insurmountable. The tipping point is usually when search fails often enough that people stop looking in Confluence first and start asking colleagues directly — at that point, the knowledge base has lost its reason to exist.
