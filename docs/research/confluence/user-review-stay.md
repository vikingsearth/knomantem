# Confluence: Why Users Stay

Based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, Software Advice, TrustRadius, and Reddit (2024-2026).

## 1. Centralized "Single Source of Truth"
- Users love Confluence as a single source of truth for all company information
- Product guides, team overviews, project roadmaps, company wiki — all in one place
- "Instead of information being scattered across emails or chats, it lives in a structured space where everyone can access and update it"
- Reduces knowledge silos between teams and departments
- Eliminates the "where did I see that?" problem for established users

## 2. Deep Jira/Atlassian Ecosystem Integration (Major Stickiness Factor)
- Seamless bidirectional Jira integration is consistently the #1 cited reason teams stay
- "Bridges project management and documentation — transforms static wikis into dynamic hubs by linking pages directly to tasks, sprints, or code repositories"
- Confluence is primarily used by customers already in the Atlassian ecosystem, creating strong lock-in
- Integration with Bitbucket, Trello, Loom, and other Atlassian products compounds the switching cost
- Teams that use Jira + Confluence together find it very hard to justify migrating

## 3. Company-Wide Collaboration
- Entire company can access and collaborate in shared workspaces
- Real-time co-editing allows multiple people to work on pages simultaneously
- "Confluence is an open and shared workspace, and that's why I love Confluence" — Capterra reviewer
- Comments (inline and page-level) enable asynchronous discussion
- @mentions and notifications keep teams informed

## 4. Easy Content Creation
- Intuitive page builder with WYSIWYG editing
- Rich template library for common document types (PRDs, meeting notes, retrospectives)
- AI-powered content generation assists with drafting
- Low barrier to entry for basic documentation tasks
- Macros extend page capabilities without technical knowledge

## 5. Page Hierarchy & Organization
- Parent/child page structure provides logical content organization
- "Really helpful for organizing information logically — saves a lot of time when you need to look up something quickly or onboard someone new"
- Spaces provide clear separation between teams/projects
- Labels add cross-cutting categorization
- Breadcrumb navigation shows where you are in the hierarchy

## 6. Value for Money
- Standard plan: $5.16/user/month (for 300 users)
- Premium: $9.73/user/month
- Significantly lower than the average SMB software budget of $150/user/month
- Free tier available for up to 10 users
- Good feature-to-price ratio compared to competitors

## 7. Versatility Across Teams
- Works for engineering (technical docs, runbooks, architecture decisions)
- Works for product (PRDs, roadmaps, specs)
- Works for HR (onboarding docs, policies, handbooks)
- Works for marketing (campaign briefs, brand guidelines)
- Works for support (knowledge bases, FAQs, troubleshooting guides)
- Adaptable to many team workflows without heavy customization

## 8. Mature & Trusted Platform
- Long track record (launched 2004, 20+ years of development)
- Large user base provides confidence in longevity
- Extensive documentation and community support
- Enterprise-grade security and compliance certifications
- Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader status provides organizational buy-in

## 9. External Sharing
- Restricted public links allow sharing specific content with external audiences
- Customer FAQs, release notes, and documentation shareable without extra licenses
- Enables knowledge bases that serve both internal and external users

## 10. Plugin Ecosystem
- Thousands of apps on the Atlassian Marketplace
- Extend Confluence with diagrams (draw.io), forms, calendars, advanced tables, and more
- Third-party integrations fill gaps in native functionality
- Active developer community building new extensions

## 11. Onboarding & Knowledge Transfer
- Particularly valued for employee onboarding documentation
- New hires can self-serve through structured knowledge bases
- Reduces time-to-productivity for new team members
- Institutional knowledge captured rather than lost when people leave

## Key Retention Insight
The strongest retention factor is ecosystem lock-in — teams using Jira + Confluence together rarely switch. The collaboration and central documentation features are valued but it's the integration web that makes Confluence truly sticky.
