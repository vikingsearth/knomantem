# How to Win: Lessons from SharePoint

Inferences drawn from SharePoint's knowledge management, graph, staleness, search, and user review research.

## What SharePoint Got Right (Steal These)

### 1. Enterprise Governance as a First-Class Feature
SharePoint's compliance capabilities (retention policies, sensitivity labels, audit trails, DLP) are genuinely best-in-class. Regulated industries can't live without them. **Lesson**: If you're targeting enterprise, governance isn't optional. Build retention, permissions, and audit trails into the foundation — not as an add-on.

### 2. Metadata as the Foundation for Everything
SharePoint's managed metadata / term store provides the structured backbone that powers search, filtering, content rollup, and AI grounding. When organizations invest in metadata, everything else works better. **Lesson**: Make metadata easy to apply and hard to ignore. Autofill columns (AI-powered metadata extraction) dramatically reduce the manual tagging burden. If you can make metadata effortless, you solve the discovery problem.

### 3. Copilot/AI Grounded in Content
Microsoft 365 Copilot grounded in SharePoint content is genuinely powerful — it turns the entire document corpus into an answerable knowledge base. **Lesson**: AI-powered Q&A grounded in your content is the future of search. Don't build a search box — build an answer engine.

### 4. Knowledge Agent Direction
The Knowledge Agent's ability to auto-fill metadata, create smart views, fix broken links, and detect content gaps represents the future of knowledge management. **Lesson**: AI should maintain the knowledge base, not just query it. Proactive maintenance (broken link detection, gap analysis, staleness flagging) is more valuable than reactive search.

## Where SharePoint Fails (Attack These Gaps)

### 5. Complexity That Kills Adoption
"An incredibly powerful platform sold as a simple solution" — this mismatch is SharePoint's fatal flaw. Users expect simplicity but get enterprise complexity. **Opportunity**: Be genuinely simple for 80% of use cases. Don't hide complexity — eliminate it. The best knowledge management tool is the one people actually use.

### 6. The "Content Swamp" Problem
Without deliberate IA investment, SharePoint becomes chaos. And most organizations don't invest. **Opportunity**: Don't require IA expertise. Use AI to organize content automatically. Suggest structure, auto-categorize, and surface organization health metrics. Make the platform self-organizing.

### 7. Viva Topics Retirement Left a Void
Entity extraction, topic cards, knowledge graphs, subject matter expert identification — all gone. **Opportunity**: This is an enormous gap in the enterprise KM market. Build what Viva Topics was, but better — and not locked into the Microsoft ecosystem.

### 8. Two-Tier Experience (Copilot vs. Non-Copilot)
Only Copilot-licensed users get AI search. Everyone else gets declining keyword search. **Opportunity**: Don't gate your best features behind premium tiers. AI-powered search should be the baseline experience, not a luxury add-on.

### 9. "Shadow Abandonment"
Organizations don't leave SharePoint — they abandon it in place. They keep paying but stop using it, routing work through Notion, Google Docs, or Slack. **Opportunity**: Target shadow-abandoned SharePoint users. They're paying for something they hate and looking for alternatives. They already have budget allocated.

### 10. Mobile Is an Afterthought
SharePoint's mobile experience is universally criticized. **Opportunity**: Mobile-first knowledge access. The knowledge you need should be available in your pocket, not just on your desktop.

## Strategic Takeaways

### You Can't Out-Microsoft Microsoft
Don't try to replicate SharePoint's breadth. Instead, be 10x better at the things SharePoint can't do well:
- Simplicity of setup and use
- Content that stays fresh automatically
- Knowledge discovery through entity extraction and graphs
- Search that works without metadata investment
- Mobile experience that's genuinely useful

### The Metadata Paradox
SharePoint proves that metadata-driven knowledge management works brilliantly — IF people tag their content. They don't. **The insight**: AI-powered automatic metadata extraction is the unlock. Eliminate the manual step entirely.

### Target the "Failed SharePoint" Market
There are millions of organizations with SharePoint deployments that aren't working. They've given up on making SharePoint useful for KM. These are your customers. The pitch: "Your SharePoint isn't working for knowledge management. We will."
